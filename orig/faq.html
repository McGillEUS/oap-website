FAQ

sooo...you want to know more?

What do I need to get into OAP?

To get into OAP you are required to be over 18 years of age and have a valid government issued photo ID and be a McGill student. Fear not if you aren't a McGill student, as you can get in as long as you are with a McGill student at the time of entry.

Can I get into OAP if I'm not a McGill student?

Yes! Fear not if you aren't a McGill student, as you can get in as long as you are with a McGill student at the time of entry.

What time does OAP close at?

OAP runs from 12PM to 9PM, Monday to Friday (except labour day, September 7th) between August 31st and September 11th. It is important to note that there is no re-entry after 8:30PM.

Where do the profits from OAP go?

All profits from the fall OAP go towards the Engineering Undergraduate Society and help in funding its many clubs and design teams which often need expensive parts and equipment to operate. All profits from OAP Lite go to the Presidients Choice Children's Charity.

What food is availible at OAP?

OAP offers beef burgers, chicken burgers, hot-dogs, and veggie burgers all grilled right before your eyes. Cheese is also availible at a small additional cost. See our menu in detail here.

What drinks are availible at OAP?

OAP offers beer for $3.50 (5 for $15), or water or pop for $1. See the full beverage menu here.

Who organizes OAP?

OAP is organized and run by a dedicated team of managers consisting of students from the Faculty of Engineering who volunteer their time to make this event happen. However OAP would not be possible without the continued dedication of hundreds of student volunteers from accross all faculties.

How can I volunteer?

Anyone can volunteer and we're always looking for people to help out! Come by the ticket tent or find a manager in a black shirt anytime and say you're interested in volunteering. If you would like to stay updated on all things OAP volunteering then join the facebook group here.

Do I need to be in engineering to volunteer?

Absolutely not! Anyone is welcome to help out so don't be shy!

I noticed the prices were cheaper last year, what gives?

Unfortunately, due to new mandated costs from McGill including porta-potties and extra security, as well as simple inflation and the desire to provide the best possible service, it has become impossible to run OAP in a sustainable manner with the current pricing system which has been in place for over a decade. This increase will guarantee the sustanibility of the event in the future and allow all McGill students to enjoy OAP for years to come.

I love OAP so much and want to rep it everywhere I go, where can I get some swag?

Come by the ticket tent, we've got you covered! We've got t-shirts, tanks, and sunglasses for super low prices! Keep an eye out for new swag to be added in the future as well.
